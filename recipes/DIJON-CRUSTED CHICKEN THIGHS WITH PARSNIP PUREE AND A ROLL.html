DIJON-CRUSTED CHICKEN THIGHS WITH PARSNIP PUREE AND A ROLL
  • 1/2 cup chopped parsley
  • 2 1/4 tsp rosemary
  • 1 1/2 tsp dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 2 1/2 tsp white wine vinegar
  • 1 2/3 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 1/4 chicken thighs
  • 1 2/3 parsnips
  • 1/2 lemon
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 1/4 tsp butter
    • Instructions:
    • [Prepare the chicken]
    • Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.
    • Chop parsley and rosemary. Combine in a large bowl with mustard, 1 2/3 tsp garlic, white wine vinegar, 3/4 tbsp olive oil, and salt and pepper to taste. Add chicken thighs and toss to coat. Set aside.
    • Heat a large oven-safe skillet over medium. Sear chicken thighs on both sides, about 2 minutes each, and transfer to oven. Roast 15 to 20 minutes, or until no longer pink in the middle.
    • [Prepare the parsnip mash]
    • Peel parsnip and cut in bite-size pieces. Zest yellow rind of the lemon with a grater or zester, avoiding the white pith; then, de-seed and juice the lemon.
    • Heat remaining 3/4 tbsp olive oil in a saucepan over medium-high. Add remaining 1 2/3 tsp garlic and the parsnip and saute 1 minute. Add milk and let simmer until soft.
    • Transfer mixture to a blender or food processor, along with butter, 3/4 tsp lemon zest and 1 2/3 tsp juice, and salt to taste. Pulse until smooth, adding more liquid (milk or water) if needed.
    • Enjoy chicken thigh with parsnip puree, with pan juices spooned on top.
